# SunPark Website Taskmaster

Status: approved through Phases 0-14. Core public pages, motion polish, and QA pass are complete.

## Purpose

Build a premium commercial website for SunPark, a Solar Carport as a Service company serving Morocco's commercial sector.

The website is not a pitch deck, not an investor memo, and not a business-plan explainer. The provided documents are context sources only. Their role is to supply accurate facts, numbers, positioning, and constraints so the site can communicate with commercial customers, property owners, partners, and serious stakeholders in a polished, credible way.

The site should feel like a high-quality infrastructure and energy services company: calm, precise, trustworthy, commercially fluent, and technically credible.

Rules:

- Do not invent facts, customers, signed contracts, case studies, team bios, office addresses, partner logos, certifications, awards, or operational history.
- Do not expose academic/business-plan scaffolding on the public website.
- Do not use phrases like "TAM", "SAM", "SOM", "valuation cap", "Series A ready", "exit ready", "IRR", "DCF", or "convertible note" in customer-facing pages unless a future investor page is explicitly approved.
- Translate source facts into client-facing benefits.
- Be transparent where needed, but do not make the website sound like a classroom submission or funding presentation.

## Client-Facing Positioning

SunPark helps commercial sites in Morocco turn existing parking areas into shaded solar infrastructure.

Customer-facing proposition:

- No upfront capital expenditure.
- SunPark finances, owns, operates, and maintains the solar carport.
- The site buys clean electricity through a long-term Power Purchase Agreement.
- The rate is set below the commercial grid benchmark used in the source documents.
- Customers gain shaded parking, lower energy costs, and a practical route to on-site renewable power.

Approved public-language themes:

- "Turn parking into power."
- "Solar carports without upfront capex."
- "Clean energy infrastructure for commercial parking."
- "A lower-cost energy contract, not a construction project."
- "Built for Morocco's commercial sites."
- "Designed for retail, logistics, hospitality, and office properties."

Avoid:

- Over-explaining the business model.
- Investor-first language.
- Academic theory references.
- Internal validation language unless framed carefully.
- Claims that imply revenue, completed installations, signed PPAs, or independent customer LOIs.

## Source-Backed Facts For Public Use

Use these facts selectively, in plain client-facing language:

- Morocco imports approximately 90% of its energy.
- Commercial grid power benchmark in the sources is approximately GBP 0.086/kWh.
- SunPark's base PPA tariff in the sources is GBP 0.069/kWh.
- Law 82-21 created a framework for distributed self-generation and third-party PPAs.
- Installations below 5 MW benefit from simplified licensing.
- Morocco has a national target of 52% installed renewable capacity by 2030.
- The SR500 programme targets 500 MW of commercial and industrial solar capacity by 2030.
- SunPark focuses on commercial parking across retail, logistics, hospitality, and office sectors.
- A typical source-modelled site uses around 300 kWp and can generate approximately 1.25M kWh/year.
- SunPark's model is designed to address three adoption barriers: upfront capex, split landlord/tenant ownership, and lack of standardised commercial PPA structures.
- The first validation site identified in the sources is an Agadir logistics warehouse through a direct relationship. Treat this carefully as a pilot candidate, not a completed public case study.

## Design System Commitments

The website must follow `DESIGN-airtable.md` exactly:

- White editorial canvas as the primary atmosphere.
- Near-black primary CTA: `#181d26`, white text, 12px radius, 16px 24px padding.
- Secondary CTA: white background, near-black text, hairline border.
- Display typography uses modest weights. No heavy bold marketing headings.
- Major sections follow 96px vertical spacing.
- Use full-card signature surfaces for moments of emphasis: coral, forest, cream, dark navy, peach, mint, yellow, mustard.
- No gradient hero, mesh background, neon glow, or generic SaaS backdrop.
- No undocumented colors.
- Minimal shadows. Depth comes from whitespace, typography, layout, and color-blocked cards.
- Pricing/investor-style pill buttons are not a general site pattern.
- Motion must feel editorial and premium, not decorative.

## Recommended Site Architecture

Primary audience:

- Landlords with parking assets.

Sections:

   - Visual direction: clean editorial whitespace, precise solar-carport linework or abstract parking-to-power composition. No fake product screenshots or stocky green-energy clichés.

4. What SunPark Builds

5. How It Works

7. Built For Commercial Sites

     - Logistics.
     - Hospitality.
     - Offices.

8. Customer Economics Preview

## Motion And Interaction Standard

Motion must feel like a world-class editorial infrastructure site, not a template.

Principles:

- Calm.
- Precise.
- Purposeful.
- Content-led.
- Reduced-motion compatible.

Required motion plan:

1. Page transitions
   - Subtle fade and vertical settle.
   - Preserve scroll position behavior intentionally.

2. Hero entrance
   - Line-by-line heading reveal.
   - Supporting copy follows.
   - CTA pair enters last.
   - No dramatic masking effects that fight the sober design system.

3. Scroll reveals
   - Benefits and sector cards enter with measured stagger.
   - Signature cards should feel heavier and slower than standard cards.

4. Process timeline
   - Progress indicator advances with scroll.
   - Current step highlights through ink contrast and spacing, not bright colors.

5. Metric treatment
   - Optional count-up for stable metrics only.
   - Do not animate financial figures in a gimmicky way.

6. Form interactions
   - Inputs use precise focus states from the design system.
   - Submission and validation states must be calm and clear.

7. Hover states
   - Keep restrained.
   - No glow, tilt, magnetic buttons, or color effects outside the design system.

8. Reduced motion
   - Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
   - No content should depend on motion to be understood.
